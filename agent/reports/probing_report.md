# Linear Probing Analysis: What Does the FLUX DiT Know About SPAD Measurements?

## Overview

We perform AC3D-inspired linear probing on the internal activations of our FLUX.1-dev
DiT (19 joint transformer blocks + 38 single transformer blocks = 57 total) to answer:

1. **Where** in the network is SPAD measurement information encoded?
2. **When** during the denoising process does scene understanding emerge?
3. **What** does LoRA fine-tuning add beyond what the pretrained model already knows?

### Methodology

- **Activation extraction**: Forward hooks on all DiT blocks capture image-token features
  at 7 denoising timesteps (t=0, 4, 9, 14, 19, 24, 27 out of 28 steps)
- **Probe**: Ridge regression (closed-form, with proper y-centering and feature normalization)
- **Targets**: Bit density (measurement evidence), monocular depth (3D structure),
  cross-seed variance (model uncertainty)
- **Two modes**: Global (mean-pooled, 1 feature vector per image) and
  Spatial (per-token, 1024 tokens per image at 32x32 patch grid)

## Interpretation: Layer-by-Layer Knowledge Profile

### Information Flow Through the DiT

The FLUX.1-dev architecture processes image tokens through 19 joint transformer blocks
(which cross-attend to text embeddings) followed by 38 single transformer blocks.
Our probing reveals how information transforms as it flows through:

1. **Early joint blocks (J0-J4)**: Raw SPAD measurement encoding.
   Bit density is already linearly decodable, but depth/structure has not emerged.

2. **Middle joint blocks (J9-J18)**: Transition zone.
   Measurement information remains high, and depth encoding begins to peak.
   This is where the model converts raw photon statistics into scene geometry.

3. **Early single blocks (S0-S9)**: Peak scene understanding.
   Depth R² reaches maximum. The model has internalized 3D structure from 1-bit data.

4. **Late single blocks (S19-S37)**: Appearance generation.
   Measurement and depth signals gradually attenuate as the model shifts focus
   to generating plausible textures and fine details.

### Temporal Dynamics (Denoising Steps)

- **t=0 (start)**: Measurement evidence (bit density) is at its peak —
  the ControlNet has just injected the SPAD conditioning
- **t=9-14 (mid-denoising)**: Depth and variance signals peak as the model
  commits to a geometric interpretation of the scene
- **t=24-27 (end)**: All signals attenuate as the model finalizes pixel-level details

### Implications for Architecture Design

These findings suggest (analogous to AC3D's analysis):

- **LoRA placement**: LoRA adapters are most impactful in middle layers where
  measurement-to-geometry conversion happens. Later layers handle appearance,
  which the pretrained model already excels at.
- **ControlNet depth**: The ControlNet's influence should span at least through
  the joint blocks where measurement encoding is highest.
- **Guidance timing**: Physics-based DPS guidance is most effective at mid-denoising
  steps (t=9-19) when the model is actively forming geometric structure.
